## Managing Blog Posts

### Creating a New Blog Post

#### Step 1: Start a New Post

1. Click **"Blog Posts"** in the left sidebar
2. Click the **"+ Create"** button
3. Select **"Blog Post"**

#### Step 2: Fill in Basic Information

**Title** (Required)
- Write your headline
- Good: "5 Ways Design Creates Equity in Education"
- Keep it between 10-100 characters
- âš ï¸ You'll see a warning if it's too short/long

**URL Slug** (Required)
- Click "Generate" next to the slug field
- It automatically creates a URL-friendly version
- Example: "5-ways-design-creates-equity" âœ“
- You can edit it if needed

**Excerpt** (Required)
- Write a 1-2 sentence summary
- This appears in previews and search results
- Aim for 50-160 characters
- Think: "What would make me click on this?"

#### Step 3: Write Your Content

The **Content** field is a rich text editor with these features:

**Formatting Options:**
- **Bold**: Select text, click **B** button
- *Italic*: Select text, click *I* button
- Headings: Use dropdown for H2, H3, H4
- Lists: Bullet or numbered

**Adding Images:**
1. Click the **ğŸ“· Image** button
2. Drag & drop or click to upload
3. **IMPORTANT**: Fill in the "Alt Text" (describes the image for accessibility)
4. Optionally add a caption

**Adding Links:**
1. Select text you want to link
2. Click the **ğŸ”— Link** button
3. Paste URL or search for internal pages
4. Choose "Open in new tab" if linking externally

**Adding Callout Boxes:**
1. Click **"+ Add block"**
2. Select **"Callout Box"**
3. Choose type: Info, Warning, Success, or Tip
4. Write your text

**Example Blog Post Content Structure:**
```
Introduction paragraph (2-3 sentences)

## Main Point 1
Supporting paragraphs...
[Image with caption]

## Main Point 2
Supporting paragraphs...
- Bullet list of key points
- Another point
- One more

[Callout Box: Key Takeaway]

## Conclusion
Wrap-up paragraph...
```

#### Step 4: Add Featured Image

1. Scroll to **"Featured Image"**
2. Click to upload or drag & drop
3. **Adjust the focal point** by dragging the circle (this ensures the important part shows in previews)
4. Fill in Alt Text

**Image Guidelines:**
- Recommended size: 1200x630px (works for social media too)
- File format: JPG or PNG
- File size: Keep under 500KB for fast loading
- Alt text example: "Students collaborating on a design project in a classroom"

#### Step 5: Add Metadata

**Author**
- Click to select yourself from the team list
- If you don't see your name, ask an admin to add you first

**Categories** (Select 1-2)
- Choose the main topic(s)
- Don't over-categorizeâ€”less is more
- Examples: "Design", "Equity", "Case Study"

**Tags** (Optional)
- Add 3-5 specific keywords
- Type and press Enter after each tag
- Examples: "accessibility", "nonprofit", "UX design"

**Published Date**
- Defaults to today
- Click to schedule for a future date
- â° The post won't appear until this date/time

#### Step 6: SEO Settings (Optional but Recommended)

Click **"SEO Settings"** to expand:

**Meta Title**
- Custom title for Google search results
- If blank, uses your post title
- Limit: 60 characters

**Meta Description**
- Custom description for search results
- If blank, uses your excerpt
- Limit: 160 characters
- Make it compellingâ€”this is your "ad" in search results!

**Focus Keywords**
- 2-4 keywords you want to rank for
- Example: "equity design", "inclusive UX"

#### Step 7: Preview & Publish

**Preview Your Post:**
1. Click **"Preview"** button (top right)
2. Opens website preview in new tab
3. Check for typos, formatting, images

**Save as Draft:**
- Click **"Save"** button
- âœ… Your work is saved
- âš ï¸ Post is NOT live yet (Draft checkbox is checked)

**Publish Your Post:**
1. Uncheck **"Draft Status"** checkbox
2. Click **"Publish"**
3. ğŸ‰ Your post is live!
4. It will appear on the website within 1-2 minutes

## Managing Case Studies

Case studies are more complex than blog posts, but the Studio makes it easy with organized tabs.

### Creating a New Case Study

1. Click **"Case Studies"** in sidebar
2. Click **"+ Create"**
3. Select **"Case Study"**

### Navigation Tabs

You'll see tabs at the top:
- **Overview**: Basic project information
- **The Challenge**: Problem statement
- **Our Approach**: Methodology and process
- **The Solution**: What was delivered
- **Impact & Results**: Outcomes and metrics
- **Media & Assets**: Images and videos
- **SEO & Settings**: Publication settings

### Overview Tab

**Project Title**
- Make it descriptive and compelling
- Good: "Redesigning Education Access for Underserved Communities"
- Avoid: "ABC Nonprofit Website"

**Client Information:**
- **Client Name**: Organization name
- **Industry**: Select from dropdown
- **Location**: City, State
- **Website**: Full URL
- **Logo**: Upload client's logo (PNG with transparent background ideal)

**Project Type** (Check all that apply)
- Brand Identity
- Website Design & Development
- UX/UI Design
- etc.

**Featured Case Study**
- Check this box to highlight on homepage
- Limit to 3 featured projects at a time

### The Challenge Tab

**Challenge Overview**
- Write 2-3 paragraphs describing the problem
- Use rich text formatting for readability
- Include context: Who? What? Why?

**Key Pain Points**
- Click "Add item" for each pain point
- Give each a clear title and description
- Aim for 3-5 pain points

**Background Context**
- Additional details that set the scene
- Optional but recommended

**Challenge Image**
- Visual representation of the problem
- Could be: "before" state, data visualization, etc.

### Our Approach Tab

**Methodology Overview**
- Describe your process at a high level
- 1-2 paragraphs

**Process Steps**
1. Click "Add item" for each step
2. Fill in:
   - **Step Number**: 1, 2, 3, etc.
   - **Title**: E.g., "Discovery & Research"
   - **Description**: What happened in this phase
   - **Duration**: E.g., "2 weeks"
   - **Key Activities**: Bullet points
   - **Icon**: Choose visual representation

**Pro Tip**: Keep steps concise. 4-6 steps is ideal.

### The Solution Tab

**Solution Overview**
- Describe what you created
- Focus on the solution, not the process
- 2-3 paragraphs

**Key Deliverables**
- List everything the client received
- Examples: "New brand identity", "Responsive website", "Content strategy"

**Notable Features**
- Bullet points of standout elements
- Keep it high-level and client-focused

### Impact & Results Tab

**This is the most important section!**

**Impact Overview**
- Write the success story
- Use specific, measurable language
- 2-3 paragraphs

**Impact Metrics** (The star of the show!)

For each metric:
1. Click "Add metric"
2. Fill in:
   - **Metric Name**: E.g., "Website Traffic"
   - **Value**: E.g., "250%"
   - **Change Direction**: Increase â†‘ / Decrease â†“ / Neutral â†’
   - **Context**: E.g., "increase in first 3 months"
   - **Icon**: Visual category

**Example Metrics:**
- "User Engagement: 85% â†‘ (increase in session duration)"
- "Donor Conversion: 3x â†‘ (tripled donation completion rate)"
- "Accessibility Score: 98/100 âœ“ (WCAG AA compliant)"

**Client Testimonial** (Highly recommended!)
- Paste the quote
- Fill in author's name and title
- Upload their photo if available

### Media & Assets Tab

**Hero Image** (Required)
- Main project image
- High-quality, showcases the work
- Recommended: 1920x1080px

**Project Gallery**
- Upload 4-10 images
- Mix of screenshots, photos, close-ups
- Each needs Alt Text
- Optional captions
- Set display order (1, 2, 3...)

**Before/After Comparison** (Optional but powerful!)
- Upload "before" image
- Upload "after" image
- Add description

**Project Videos** (Optional)
- Paste YouTube or Vimeo URL
- Add title
- Upload custom thumbnail if desired

### SEO & Settings Tab

**Published Date**: When to make it live
**Draft Status**: Uncheck to publish
**Related Case Studies**: Link to similar projects (auto-suggests)

## Managing Team Members

### Adding a New Team Member

1. Click **"Team Members"**
2. Click **"+ Create"**

### Essential Fields

**Full Name**: First and last name
**Job Title/Role**: E.g., "Lead Designer", "Creative Director"
**Department**: Select from dropdown
**Display Order**:
- Lower numbers appear first
- Leadership: 1-10
- Staff: 11+

**Profile Photo**
- Square crop recommended
- High-quality headshot
- Professional but warm

**Short Bio** (Shown on team grid)
- One sentence about them
- Example: "Sarah brings 10 years of UX expertise to equity-focused nonprofits."

**Full Bio** (Shown on individual profile)
- 2-3 paragraphs
- Can include:
  - Background
  - Expertise
  - What drives them
  - Personal touch

**Areas of Expertise** (Tags)
- Type each skill and press Enter
- Examples: "UX Research", "Brand Strategy", "Accessibility"
- Limit to 5-8 most relevant

### Optional But Nice-to-Have

**Personal Quote**: A mantra or philosophy
**Pronouns**: she/her, he/him, they/them
**Years of Experience**: Number
**Social Links**: LinkedIn, Twitter, portfolio site
**Languages Spoken**: Especially relevant for diverse teams
**Personal Interests**: Humanizes the profile

### Making Changes

**To update a team member:**
1. Find them in the list
2. Click to open
3. Make edits
4. Click "Publish"

**To remove from website (but keep in system):**
- Uncheck **"Active Team Member"**
- They'll be hidden from the site but remain in the database

**To change order on team page:**
- Adjust **"Display Order"** number
- Lower numbers = appears first

## Managing Services

### Creating a New Service

1. Click **"Services"**
2. Click **"+ Create"**

### Overview Tab

**Service Name**: E.g., "Brand Identity Design"
**Tagline**: One compelling sentence
**Description**: Brief overview (1-2 sentences)
**Icon**: Choose visual representation
**Category**: Design, Development, Strategy, etc.
**Featured**: Check to highlight on services page

### Details Tab

**Full Description**
- Rich text content
- Explain what this service is
- Who it's for
- What problems it solves

**Ideal For**
- Bullet points
- E.g., "Nonprofits launching new initiatives"
- Be specific about your target audience

**Key Capabilities**
- What you can do within this service
- Each capability gets a name and description

**Typical Deliverables**
- What clients receive
- Concrete, specific items

**Key Benefits**
- Value propositions
- Focus on outcomes, not activities
- E.g., "Increased donor engagement" not "We design forms"

### Process Tab

**Process Overview**: Introduction to your methodology

**Process Steps**:
1. Add each phase of your process
2. Fill in: Number, Title, Description, Duration, Activities, Icon
3. Recommended: 4-8 steps

**Typical Timeline**:
- Minimum duration
- Typical duration
- Notes on what affects timeline

### Pricing Tab (Optional)

**Pricing Model**: Fixed, Hourly, Retainer, etc.
**Starting Price**: E.g., "$5,000" or "Contact for pricing"
**Pricing Details**: Additional context

**Service Packages** (Optional):
- Create tiers: Essential, Professional, Premium
- For each: Name, Price, Description, Features
- Mark one as "Recommended"

## Working with Images

### Uploading Images

**Method 1: Drag & Drop**
1. Drag image from your desktop
2. Drop into the image field
3. It uploads automatically

**Method 2: Click to Upload**
1. Click the image field
2. Browse your computer
3. Select image
4. Click Open

### Image Best Practices

**File Formats:**
- âœ… JPG for photos
- âœ… PNG for graphics with transparency
- âœ… SVG for logos (if available)
- âŒ Avoid: BMP, TIFF

**File Sizes:**
- Aim for < 500KB
- Large images slow down the website
- Use [TinyPNG.com](https://tinypng.com) to compress before uploading

**Dimensions:**
- Featured images: 1200x630px (works for social media)
- Gallery images: 1920x1080px
- Team photos: 800x800px (square)
- Logos: Any size (they'll be resized)

**Alt Text** (Always required!)
- Describes the image for:
  - Screen readers (accessibility)
  - SEO (search engines)
  - If image doesn't load
- Good: "Team members collaborating around a whiteboard with sticky notes"
- Bad: "Image 1", "Photo", "IMG_1234"

## Publishing Workflow

### Draft â†’ Review â†’ Publish

#### Working with Drafts

**When you're creating content:**
1. The "Draft Status" checkbox is checked by default
2. âœ… Content is saved
3. âš ï¸ Content is NOT visible on the website
4. You can keep working and saving without affecting the live site

**Benefits of Drafts:**
- Write in multiple sessions
- Get feedback before publishing
- Schedule content in advance

#### Preview Before Publishing

**Always preview first!**

1. Click **"Preview"** button (top right)
2. Opens your website with the draft content
3. Check:
   - âœ… No typos
   - âœ… Images look good
   - âœ… Links work
   - âœ… Formatting is correct
   - âœ… Reads well on mobile (resize your browser)

#### Publishing

**To publish:**
1. Uncheck **"Draft Status"**
2. Verify **"Published Date"** (now or future?)
3. Click **"Publish"**
4. âœ¨ Content goes live within 1-2 minutes

**To schedule for later:**
1. Keep "Draft Status" checked
2. Set **"Published Date"** to future date/time
3. Click "Save"
4. Uncheck "Draft Status" and publish when ready

#### Unpublishing

**To remove published content:**
1. Open the content item
2. Check **"Draft Status"** again
3. Click **"Publish"**
4. Content is now hidden from the website (but still in the system)

## Tips & Best Practices

### Content Creation Tips

**âœï¸ Writing:**
- Write in short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Use headings to break up content
- Use bullet points for lists
- Write in active voice: "We designed..." not "It was designed..."
- Read aloudâ€”if you stumble, readers will too

**ğŸ¨ Visual Content:**
- Every piece of content should have at least one image
- Use high-quality, relevant images
- Avoid stock photos that feel generic
- Show real people, real work, real impact

**â™¿ Accessibility:**
- Always write alt text for images
- Use heading hierarchy correctly (H2 â†’ H3, not H2 â†’ H4)
- Make links descriptive ("Read our case study" not "Click here")
- Ensure good color contrast

**ğŸ“± Mobile-First:**
- Most visitors are on mobile
- Keep paragraphs short
- Use images that work vertically
- Test preview on your phone

### SEO Best Practices

**For Every Post/Page:**
- [ ] Compelling title (with keywords)
- [ ] Clear, engaging excerpt/description
- [ ] Featured image with alt text
- [ ] At least 300 words of content
- [ ] 1-2 internal links to other pages
- [ ] External links open in new tabs
- [ ] Custom meta description (if different from excerpt)

**Keywords:**
- Research what your audience searches for
- Include keywords naturally in:
  - Title
  - First paragraph
  - Headings
  - Alt text
  - Meta description
- Don't stuff keywordsâ€”write for humans first
